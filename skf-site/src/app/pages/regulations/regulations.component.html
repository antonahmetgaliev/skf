General Regulations

  1. These regulations apply to all official SKF Racing Hub events (single events, series, leagues) across all simulators.
  2. The purpose of these regulations is to ensure fair, safe, and comfortable participation for all drivers.
  3. Participants are required to follow these regulations and the decisions of the administration/stewards.
  4. The organizer reserves the right to make changes to the regulations with prior notification.

Participants and Registration

  1. Participation in any SKF Racing Hub event is only available to members of our Discord server. All communication, briefings, and announcements are conducted exclusively there.

  2. Registration for events is done via a special form or a specified Discord channel. After registration, the driver receives confirmation and, if necessary, the server password.
  3. The in-game name must match the name in the registration form and on Discord. Changing the nickname during the season or event without notifying the administration is prohibited.
  4. If a driver cannot participate in a race, they must notify the administration at least 2 hours before the start through Discord.
  5. The administration reserves the right to deny participation to players in case of repeated rule violations, toxic behavior, or technical issues (high ping, unstable FPS, etc.).

Requirements

3.1. Software Requirements

  1. Participants must have an active subscription or access to the relevant simulator (LMU, iRacing, ACC) and the updated version of the game.
  2. Participants must own the car and track used in the event or series.
  3. Attendance at the briefing in the SKF Racing Hub Discord is mandatory during official events.

3.2. Driver Names and Numbers

  1. The in-game name must match the Discord and registration name. Offensive, obscene, or provocative names are prohibited.
  2. Numbers are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis.
  3. If two drivers from different classes request the same number, priority goes to the one who registered first.
  4. Number range: 000–999.

3.3. Team Names

  1. Team names must comply with ethical standards and cannot include offensive or provocative content.
  2. Violations may result in removal from the event or a ban from future participation.

Competition Format

  1. Event format (time, session duration, number of races, track type, car classes) is published separately for each event.
  2. Race start procedures follow the regulations of the specific series.

On-Track Behavior

5.1. General Rules

  1. The race start time is considered the start of the Practice session preceding the Race.
  2. Only the race director can decide on a session delay or restart.
  3. Intentional or accidental actions that cause contact for advantage are prohibited.
  4. Drivers must leave enough space for opponents to avoid incidents leading to loss of time, positions, or significant damage.
  5. Unsportsmanlike behavior is punishable by penalties, including removal from the race, championship, or community.
  6. Formation Lap: Maintain a safe distance; ghosting is disabled. Incidents during the warm-up lap will result in penalties.

5.2. Qualification

5.2.1. Solo Qualification

  1. Drive alone without other cars on the track.
  2. The goal is to set your best lap time.

5.2.2. Open Qualification

  1. Overtaking is allowed only if the car ahead completely vacates the line.
  2. Cars on a flying lap are not required to yield unless they receive a blue flag or abandon their lap.
  3. Returning to the pits via the menu is allowed only after safely stopping off the racing line.
  4. Stopping on the racing line that causes contact may be penalized. The maximum possible penalty is disqualification from the current or the next race.

5.2.3. Multiclass Qualification

  1. Each class has its own time slot.
  2. You may complete any number of laps during the allocated time slot.
  3. Crossing the start/finish line before the allocated time expires and the simulator allows it, the driver has the right to complete their timed lap.
  4. Exceeding your time slot or interfering with another class leads to penalties (up to starting from the pit lane).

5.3. Blue Flag

5.3.1. Actions of the Lapped Driver

  1. A driver shown a blue flag must not defend against the overtaking car.
  2. Maintain a predictable line without sudden changes of direction.
  3. Allow the faster car to pass with minimal resistance and without risk.
  4. Deliberate blocking of the faster car will be penalized.
  5. The blue flag is advisory, meaning the lapped driver has the right to maintain their racing line without altering their trajectory.

5.3.2. Actions of the Faster Driver

  1. May flash headlights to indicate intent to overtake.
  2. Must execute the overtaking maneuver safely, considering the slower car's position and line.

5.4. Multiclass and Endurance Race Features

  1. In sprint races, it is recommended for lapped cars to let faster cars by as soon as it is safe.
  2. In endurance races, it is recommended to allow faster classes to pass safely.

5.5. Race Start

  1. All events begin with a formation lap to warm up the tires and brakes. Depending on the track, the formation lap may be shortened to a small section from the starting line.
  2. Penalties for the following rules will be automatically applied by the simulator:
    1. Before the final corner, all drivers must form a double file and maintain a gap of no more than two car lengths.
    2. Drivers out of position will receive a penalty.
    3. The pole position driver must maintain the simulator-assigned speed until the green flag.
    4. Jump starts or deliberate braking of the field will result in a penalty.
    5. Multiclass:
      1. The faster class (e.g., Hypercar) starts first.
      2. The next class maintains a 10-second gap.
      3. Drivers starting from the pit lane must wait until all cars have passed the pit exit.

5.6. Race Finish

  1. After crossing the finish line, either drive to the pit lane or stop safely off the racing line and return to the pits via the menu.
  2. Race rules still apply after the finish, and penalties can be issued for violations.

5.7. Early Race Termination

  1. You may retire from the race at any time, but:
    1. Safely pull off the track outside the racing line.
    2. Return to the pits via the menu before leaving the session.

5.8. Pit Stops and Strategy

  1. Number of pit stops and requirements are specified separately for each event.
  2. Blocking pit lane entry/exit is prohibited.
  3. Entering/exiting pits must be done safely, respecting lane markings.

On-Track Behavior — Racing Conduct

6.1. General Principles

  1. Right to the Racing Line: The driver in front has the right to choose any racing line. This right is lost once the overtaking driver's front bumper reaches the level of the defending car's rear axle.
  2. Overtaking Responsibility: Both drivers — the one initiating the maneuver and the one being overtaken — share responsibility for ensuring a safe overtake.
  3. Contacts: Drivers must make every effort to avoid any contact during side-by-side racing (see Section 9).

6.2. Defending Position

  1. Only one defensive move on the straight is allowed (at least one car width).
  2. The defending driver may hold this line until the corner or return to the racing line before the turn-in point.
  3. Multiple blocking moves, changes of line while braking, and weaving to break the slipstream are prohibited.

6.3. Prohibited Actions

  1. Brake check (deliberate sudden braking to provoke contact).
  2. Punting (forcibly pushing another car off the racing line).
  3. Changing line during braking.
  4. Excessive flashing of headlights (penalized).
  5. Using pit lane without observing the pit entry speed limit. A penalty for the violation will be automatically applied by the simulator.
  6. Unsafe re-entry to the track after going off.

6.4. Vehicle Control

  1. In case of loss of control or spin, hold the brakes until it is safe to rejoin.
  2. Any uncontrolled rollback onto the racing line that causes an incident will be penalized severely.
  3. Before entering the braking zone, the defending driver must commit to a line and not change it while defending.

6.5. Bump Drafting and Close Racing

  1. Bump drafting is allowed, but if it results in an incident, the responsible driver will receive a penalty.
  2. During side-by-side racing, avoid maneuvers that could cause the opponent to lose control.

6.6. Network-Related Situations

  1. “Netcode” or game glitches will be reviewed separately, but in cases of doubt, decisions will favor the defending driver.
  2. With unstable internet, maintain greater distance and avoid risky maneuvers.

6.7. Rejoining the Track

  1. Any accident caused by unsafe rejoining will result in a penalty.

Track Limits

  1. Determined by the in-game penalty system.
  2. Exiting the pit lane only within track limits (all four wheels inside lines).
  3. Gaining an advantage or position by cutting the track is penalized regardless of automatic in-game penalties.

Incident and Penalty System

  1. All contacts, off-tracks, and pit lane violations are logged by the game and reviewed by stewards.
  2. SKF Racing Hub uses the Behavior Warning Points (BWP) system.
  3. Penalties for Accumulated BWP:
    • 6 points — Disqualification from the next qualifying session.
    • 9 points — Stop & Go penalty (SG) in the next race.
    • 12 points — Suspension from the next race.
  4. Validity Period of Points:
    1. Points remain active for 3 months.
    2. If the threshold for suspension is not met, points begin to expire after 3 months.
  5. Drivers may submit a protest regarding an incident through the official channel (Discord/form) within 24 hours after the finish.
  6. The stewards' decisions are final and cannot be appealed, except in cases explicitly provided for by these regulations.

Incident Reviews and Penalties

9.1. Incident Reviews

  1. Stewards review all incidents.
  2. Drive Through (DT) or Stop & Go (SG) penalties must be served within 3 laps or will convert to a time penalty.
  3. Post-race time penalties are added to the race result if not served during the race.
  4. Final results are published after all penalties are applied.

9.2. Penalty Severity Tiers

Stewards may apply additional penalties after the race depending on the severity of the incident:

  • Warning: Warning or no further action. 0–1 BWP
  • Minor Penalty: +5 seconds or +15 seconds. 1–2 BWP
  • Major Penalty: +30 seconds and 2–5 BWP
  • Severe Penalty: Drive Through (DT) / Stop & Go 30 (SG30) or +30 / +45 / +60 seconds added to race time. 5–10 BWP, depending on severity.
  1. Severe penalties may also include disqualification, qualifying disqualification, or 6+ BWP.

9.3. Filing Protests and Appeals

  1. Deadline: within 24 hours after the race in Discord.
  2. You must follow the instructions provided in the designated channel.
  3. You are required to save your own replay — other drivers are not obligated to share their replay files with you.
  4. A protest submission must include:
    • Event
    • Lap number
    • Turn(s)
    • Name of the driver you believe is at fault
    • A brief description of the incident
    • A replay with the HUD enabled
  5. It is recommended to use Unlisted YouTube or another video hosting platform.
  6. At least two stewards must review the incident.
  7. A protest can be filed even by a driver who was not directly involved in the incident.
  8. Using other channels or direct messages for appeals is prohibited and may result in rejection of the complaint or an additional penalty.
  9. Protests submitted after the deadline will not be considered.

Communication and Discipline

  1. Any offensive behavior, toxicity, or provocation in chat or voice channels is prohibited.
  2. Push-to-talk or a muted microphone is mandatory during sessions unless in an emergency.
  3. Administrators may remove participants for severe violations.

Force Majeure

  1. In case of server issues, the organizer may delay or restart the session.
  2. Drivers disconnected may rejoin unless prohibited by the format.
  3. If most participants disconnect, the session may be restarted or results taken from the incident moment.

Final Provisions

  1. Participation in events implies automatic agreement with these regulations.
  2. Any issues not covered by the regulations will be resolved at the discretion of the administration.
